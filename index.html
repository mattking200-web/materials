üèóÔ∏è Unit D2.2: Structures & Support

Objects That Hold Things Up!

What is a Structure?

A structure is an object that is designed to support a load. A load is anything that needs to be held up or carried. Structures help hold things up so they don't fall down!

Examples of Structures

üè† House

What it supports: The roof, walls, and everything inside
How: Strong walls and beams hold up the roof

üåâ Bridge

What it supports: Cars, trucks, and people crossing over
How: Strong beams and cables hold up the road

ü™ë Chair

What it supports: People sitting down
How: Four legs hold up the seat

üìö Bookshelf

What it supports: Books and objects
How: Strong shelves held by the frame

Parts of a Structure

Many structures have these parts:

  • Foundation: The bottom part that sits on the ground üèóÔ∏è
  • Frame: The skeleton or bones that give it shape ü¶¥
  • Supports: Parts that hold things up (like legs or beams) ü™µ
  • Covering: The outside part (like walls or skin) üß±

Strong vs. Weak Structures

üí™ Strong Structures:

‚Ä¢ Have thick, sturdy parts
‚Ä¢ Use strong materials like metal or wood
‚Ä¢ Have good support (like many legs)
‚Ä¢ Can hold heavy loads

üòü Weak Structures:

‚Ä¢ Have thin or broken parts
‚Ä¢ Use weak materials
‚Ä¢ Don't have enough support
‚Ä¢ Can break or fall easily
